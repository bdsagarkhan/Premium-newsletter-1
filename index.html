The Big Impact

Assume you sold 10 lots of Nifty at Rs. 75 per lot a week before expiry. On this you paid a brokerage
of Rs. 20. Now, on expiry, your option is at-the-money at 3:10 pm and is priced at Rs. 7.5 per lot.
However, it finally expires out-of-the money.

Scenario 1: Exit at 3:10 pm

Profit per lot = Rs 75 – Rs 7.5 = Rs 67.5 per lot

Brokerage = 20*2 (both legs of the trade) = Rs. 40

Net profit = (67.5*10 lots) – 40 = Rs. 635

Scenario 2: Let the position expire

Profit per lot = Rs 75 – Rs 0.0 = Rs 75 per lot

Brokerage = 20 (one leg of the trade) = Rs. 20

Net profit = (75*10 lots) – 20 = Rs. 730

The above given profit will increase if you consider the savings gained
due to no statutory charges or no extra charges.
